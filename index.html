All About the Boston Bruins

History

The Boston Bruins were founded in 1924 and were the first American team to be a part of the National Hockey League. They are a part of the Origanal 6 teams, which are the first 6 teams in the history of the National Hockey League. They have won the Stanley Cup 6 times in 1929, 1939, 1941, 1970, 1972, and 2011. They are a member of the Eastern Conference and in the Atlantic division. They have had a total of 51 hall of famers play on their team throughout their history and have been home to some of the greatest players in NHL history, including Booby Orr, Ray Bourque, and Cam Neely.

The owner of the Boston Bruins is Jeremy Jacobs and the general manager is Don Sweeney. Their current head coach is Bruce Cassidy who has been in charge since 2017. Their captain since 2020 is Patrice Bergeron. They are currently in 3rd place in the Atlantic division and are currently projected to make the playoffs for the 2021-2022 season. They play at the TD Garden in downtown Boston, which can hold 17,565 people per game.
